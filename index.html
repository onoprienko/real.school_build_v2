LETTER FROM COURSE CO-AUTHOR

I’m here after overcoming several burnouts and break down with the only one reason - to share helpful tools for daily life in order to deal with uncertainty, better adapt to changes in life, make choices, live your life.

My career started from the girl who is giving leaflets on the street, shifted to business developer position, later I joined the startup, then I was leading my startup. Also I worked as a consultant for international corporations as a innovation manager, leading R&D teams. And finally, could you imagine, while we were creating this online guide: I got a job offer which I had in my dreams for several years. And I declined... with strong believe that my way is here, and its the only right way.

May be you heard such a phrase: if you want to predict the future, just create it. For a long time I was controlling, oh sorry, creating my future... And I was blaming myself so hard and feel shame that I can’t be productive 24/7, that I don’t fulfill my goals so fast and so perfect as… I wanted… I couldn't control people and all events in my life . At university, business school I was taught how to achieve and survive but never how to adapt to changes in real life.

So when I was getting advice like let go, take it easy, accept, change your mindset...that was all ….bulshit for me! What does it mean on practice?

Together with different teachers and also Jazz I started my exploration. And I’m glad to introduce him to you as my trusted guide. Together with him we created the approach for solving problems and adapting to changes.

Alyona Kudko

Alyona, Head of Integrity. Member of R&D. Responsible for: new people integration & adaptation. Research community leader.

Hi Alyona! What is the most important thing in your job nowadays?

Summarizing my 12 years of experience in business, the public sector and in public organizations, I can proudly say that the most important factors for me were people and building a system of relations between them.

How did you start working with mindfulness practices?

I am a sociologist by my first education and it is this specialty that inspired in me a passion for studying people and the world around me. I finished my second magistracy in public administration, which gave me an understanding of the functioning of the state and its administration.

Having listened to my spiritual impulse, I left to engage in volunteering and work in non-profit organizations. For 2 years I have been involved in social adaptation of veterans of the anti-therroristical organisation, coordinated the work of organization volunteers and established cooperation with government bodies within the project. But after two years of work, I burned out and could not put more soul into the work that I was doing. After that, I found myself in a project that made it possible to pump my soft skills in project management, crowdfunding and leadership development, etc., to the teams in working on social projects in their regions. Inspired by this experience, I enrolled one of the most prestigious universities of Ukraine - the Ukrainian Catholic University - to the master's program on non-trademark administration. And it was this education that gave me a huge message for further self-development. My last contribution to the NGO sector was work in a project aimed at developing regional media.

What was the next step? Tell us more!

After 5 years of work in an NGO, I realized that I was ready to move to a “new level” and now I am part of the course team, I also continue volunteer work and create an alumni of my faculty at Ukrainian Catholic University and do not stop my personal development as a person and professional.

What moves you on in your daily work?

“I believe that to create some impact in this world, you have to start it from yourself, from the point of your own experience, and thereby communicate with the world. That is why it is so important for me to be the part of REAL school and contribute to its development.”
